GP-Aligner: Unsupervised Non-rigid Groupwise Point Set Registration Based on Optimized Geometric Latent Descriptor

Lingjing Wang1
Xiang Li1
Yi Fang1

1New York University


In this paper, we propose a novel method named GP-Aligner to deal with the problem of non-rigid groupwise point set registration. In comparison to previous non-learning methods, our proposed model is built upon a deep neural network-based structure, thus enables our model to align a large number of highly deformed shapes with great efficiency. Unlike most learning-based methods, our model leverages a model-free learnable latent descriptor to learn the group relationship other than using a explicit feature encoding network. More specifically, we first define an optimizable Geometric Latent Descriptor (GLD) to characterize the gruopwise relationship among a group of point sets. Each GLD is randomly initialized from a Gaussian distribution, and then concatenated with the coordinates of each point of the associated point set. A neural network-based decoder is further constructed to predict the coherent drifts as the desired transformation. GP-Aligner jointly updates all GLDs and weight parameters of the decoder network towards the minimization of an unsupervised groupwise alignment loss. During the optimization process, without specifying a target template shape, our model coherently drives each individual point set towards a middle, common position (shape). GP-Aligner does not require large-scale training data for network training and it can directly align a group of point sets in a one-stage optimization process. GP-Aligner shows both accuracy and computational efficiency improvement in comparison with state-of-the-art methods for groupwise point set registration.
