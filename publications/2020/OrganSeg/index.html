Multi-Organ Segmentation in Head and Neck CT Image with Adversarial Performance Validator

Haoyu Fang1,3
Xiaofeng Yang4
Yi Fang1,2,3

1NYU Multimedia and Visual Computing Lab
2New York University Abu Dhabi
3New York University Tandon School of Engineering
4Emory University


Human organ segmentation based on CT scan images is both of paramount importance and practical relevance in the field of medical image analysis, especially for organs-at-risk (OARs) in head-and-neck (H&N) tissue. With the progress of advance convolution neural networks, increasing efforts to introduce deep learning networks on organ segmentation in medical data are made, which results in ascending of segmentation accuracy in H&N CT images. However, the low CT contrast between OARs and their surrounding soft tissue still challenges most existing Learning-based segmentation methods. Automated organ segmentation methods based on deep learning frameworks roughly consist of 2D segmentation networks, which do not exploit Z-axis information sufficiently and 3D segmentation networks that generally consume vast computational resource. In the work, we proposed a 2D-3D hybrid segmentation network in a coarse-to-fine manner with a novel Adversaria Performance Validator (APV), which refines segmentation by penalizing residual organ information after a soft masking process, to achieve accurate and detailed segmentation for the OARs.
