In the current COVID-19 situation and during other pandemics, social distancing is a key tool for combating the rise of infections and ensuring healthcare infrastructures do not collapse from an in ux of patients. However, it is challenging for the visually impaired to practice social distancing, as their low vision prevents them from reliably maintaining a safe physical distance from other humans or crowds. In this paper, we propose a smartphone-based lightweight deep neural network (DNN) to detect human crowds and relay the associated risks to the Blind or Visually Impaired (BVI) user through directional audio alerts. Our system uses monocular images from a BVI user's smartphone to relay the risks posed to the user based on the proximity (distance from the user), the density of crowds (number of people in the crowd), and the motion of the crowd (moving towards or away from the user). The system rst detects humans and estimates their distances from the smartphone camera feed. Then, the system clusters humans into crowds to generate density and distance maps from the crowd centers. Finally, the system tracks detections in previous frames to create motion maps to predict the motion of the crowd and generate an appropriate audio alert. Active Crowd Analysis uses a computationally ecient human detection, localization, and distance regression DNN, designed for real-time smartphone use using the phone's native CPU System on Chip (SoC) hardware.
